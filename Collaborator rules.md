## Communication Standards

### 1.1 Channel Usage

| Channel | Purpose | Response Time |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| **Slack/Instant Messaging** | Quick updates, non-urgent questions | 2-4 hours |
| **Email** | Formal documentation, decisions, records | 24 hours |
| **Pull Request Comments** | Code review feedback, technical discussion | 24 hours |
| **Meeting/Sync** | Complex topics, alignment, planning | As scheduled |
| **GitHub Issues** | Task tracking, bug reports, features | 48 hours |

### 1.2 Writing Guidelines

- Use clear, concise language—avoid ambiguity
- Proofread messages before sending, especially in formal channels
- Use threads to keep conversations organized
- Include context when asking questions (what you've tried, what you expect)
- Avoid ALL CAPS unless emphasizing critical issues
- Use professional tone; avoid excessive casual language or emojis in formal contexts

### 1.3 Async-First Communication

- Assume team members may be in different time zones
- Provide detailed written context for discussions
- Do not require immediate responses; plan accordingly
- Schedule synchronous meetings only when necessary
- Record important decisions in a centralized location

## Conduct & Professionalism

### 2.1 Core Principles

- **Respect:** Treat all team members with dignity and respect regardless of role, experience, or background
- **Accountability:** Own your commitments and communicate proactively if blockers arise
- **Inclusivity:** Ensure all voices are heard; avoid dominating discussions
- **Transparency:** Share information openly; avoid hidden agendas
- **Constructive Feedback:** Focus on ideas and improvements, not personal criticism

### 2.2 Expected Behavior

✅ **Do:**
- Ask clarifying questions before making assumptions
- Give credit where credit is due
- Admit mistakes and focus on solutions
- Support team members' professional growth
- Celebrate team wins and individual contributions
- Maintain confidentiality of sensitive information

❌ **Don't:**
- Speak negatively about team members behind their backs
- Dismiss ideas without consideration
- Blame others for failures
- Take credit for others' work
- Interrupt or talk over colleagues in meetings
- Share confidential or private information

### 2.3 Availability & Reliability

- Maintain consistent working hours and communicate any changes
- Respond to critical issues within 1-2 hours during work hours
- Notify the team if taking time off; ensure coverage is arranged
- Complete commitments on time or communicate delays in advance
- Update status on assigned tasks regularly (at least twice weekly)

## Code of Conduct

### 3.1 Respectful Collaboration

This team is committed to providing a harassment-free environment. This includes:

- No discrimination based on race, gender, age, sexual orientation, disability, or other protected characteristics
- No sexual harassment, intimidation, or hostile behavior
- No bullying, public shaming, or personal attacks
- No exclusionary behavior based on background or experience level

### 3.2 Inclusive Practices

- Welcome diverse perspectives and experiences
- Use inclusive language; avoid colloquialisms that exclude
- Accommodate accessibility needs (screen readers, captions, etc.)
- Ensure junior members have opportunities to contribute and grow

### 3.3 Violations

Violations of the Code of Conduct will be addressed through the Escalation Procedures (see Section 8).

## Conflict Resolution

### 4.1 Disagreement Framework

Disagreements are healthy; how we handle them matters.

**Step 1: Direct Conversation**
- Discuss concerns privately with the involved party
- Use "I" statements: "I'm concerned about..." instead of "You always..."
- Listen to understand, not to respond
- Focus on the issue, not the person

**Step 2: Involve a Neutral Party**
- If unresolved, bring in a team lead or mediator
- Present facts and desired outcomes
- Work toward a mutually acceptable solution

**Step 3: Escalation**
- If still unresolved, escalate to management (see Section 8)

### 4.2 Technical Disagreements

- Support decisions with data and reasoning
- Be open to alternative approaches
- Consensus is preferred; escalate only when necessary
- Document final decisions for future reference

## Response Time Expectations

### 5.1 SLA by Priority

| Priority | Definition | Response Time | Resolution Time |
|----------|-----------|---|---|
| **Critical** | System down, data loss risk, security issue | 15-30 min | 2-4 hours |
| **High** | Major feature broken, blocking work | 1-2 hours | 1 business day |
| **Medium** | Feature partially working, minor impact | 4-8 hours | 2-3 business days |
| **Low** | Nice-to-have, cosmetic, no user impact | 24 hours | 1 week |

### 5.2 Out of Office

- Set your status clearly in Slack/calendar
- Designate a backup for critical tasks
- Return messages within 24 hours of returning to work

## Meeting Etiquette

### 6.1 Before the Meeting

- Review agenda and materials in advance
- Come prepared with relevant information
- Test technical setup 5-10 minutes early (for remote meetings)
- Decline if you cannot contribute meaningfully

### 6.2 During the Meeting

- Arrive on time; respect others' schedules
- Silence notifications; avoid multitasking
- Keep camera on (if applicable) to build connection
- Participate actively; don't dominate
- Use chat respectfully; avoid side conversations
- Respect agreed time limits

### 6.3 After the Meeting

- Distribute notes/action items within 24 hours
- Assign clear owners to action items with deadlines
- Follow up on decisions in written documentation

## Documentation Standards

### 7.1 Requirements

- Document decisions with reasoning and context
- Maintain a shared knowledge base (wiki, docs, etc.)
- Keep documentation current; flag outdated info
- Use clear language; avoid jargon without explanation
- Include examples where helpful

### 7.2 What to Document

- Technical decisions and architecture
- Processes and workflows
- Onboarding information
- Meeting outcomes and decisions
- Known issues and workarounds

## Escalation Procedures

### 8.1 When to Escalate

- Unresolved conflicts after direct conversation
- Code of Conduct violations
- Missed commitments or performance issues
- Concerns about team dynamics or management

### 8.2 Escalation Path

1. **Peer/Team Level:** Address with involved party first
2. **Team Lead/Manager:** Bring to direct manager or team lead
3. **HR/Leadership:** Escalate sensitive matters (harassment, discrimination, etc.)
4. **Executive:** Unresolved issues at previous levels

### 8.3 Confidentiality

- Escalations are treated confidentially
- Only relevant parties will be involved
- Retaliation for good-faith escalations is strictly prohibited
